About


I'm a Master's in Robotics student at Northeastern University, currently also working as a research assistant at Northeastern's Field Robotics Lab. I am proficient in coding with Python and C++, and I cover the spectrum of sensing, computer vision, SLAM and optimization. I have practical experience working with diverse sensors like cameras, IMUs, GPS, and lidars.

As a part of my current research at the Field Robotics Lab, I have worked on hardware and software system design to develop a multi-UAV swarm system. I have also developed the control architecture for this swarm system to achieve coordinated formation flying and target tracking. Multiple field tests and missions involved gathering precisely time-synced datasets from all drones, each equipped with multiple cameras.

Outside of my professional interests, I enjoy working out, watching movies, and playing keyboard.
